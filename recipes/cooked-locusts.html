Cooked Locusts

When are plagues of locusts not considered a nuisance? When they're a local delicacy, of course!

Well, plagues of anything will always be a nuisance, but at least this one doubles as a crunchy, Kosher snack too.
Locusts are the only insect considered Kosher and are a major source of protein - so Israelis kill two birds with one stone by eradicating these pests by eating them.

Ingredients

  • About 25 locusts

Steps

  1. Have ready about 2 liters of vegetable stock (or 10 cups) with a little turmeric added to it.
  2. Throw the locusts in the boiling stock, whole. Cook for about 3 minutes.
  3. Drain the locusts and let them cool somewhat.
  4. Twist off their heads: this will also pull out the black, threadlike viscera.
  5. Remove the wings and small legs.
  6. Make a seasoned flour with:
    • 4 tablespoons any flour
    • 3/4 teaspoon salt
    • A little pepper and chili powder
    • A shake of ground coriander
    • Dried garlic granules.
  7. Roll the pre-cooked locusts in a beaten whole egg, then roll them in the seasoned flour. Shake excess flour off.
  8. Fry in olive oil for 1 1/2-2 minutes, till color turns golden brown.
  9. Serve with a lemony tahini sauce, or a za'atar pesto made more lemony than usual.

Crunch! Enjoy!
